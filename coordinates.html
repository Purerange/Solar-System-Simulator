Azimuth: Longitudinal coordinate from observer's point of view standing on Earth, starting from North going clockwise. Goes from 0 to 360 degrees. Example: West would be 270 degrees.

Altitude: Latitudinal coordinate from observer's point of view standing on Earth, with 0 degrees being the horizon and zenith being 90 degrees. Goes from -90 to 90 degrees. A negative value means the object is below ground and cannot be seen.
